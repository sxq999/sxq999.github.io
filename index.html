Research

Target: ICCAD 2026  ·  First Author Under Preparation

Tile-Based n-Dimensional FFT Accelerator with Layer-First Cross-Dimensional Fusion for Edge Deployment

Addresses the memory-wall bottleneck in multi-dimensional FFT accelerators deployed on edge sensing systems — 2D ultrasound, 3D imaging radar, 4D MIMO radar. Proposes a provably correct tile-based scheduling algorithm that collapses off-chip DRAM traversals for arbitrary-dimensional FFT workloads, validated through cycle-accurate RTL simulation and logic synthesis on SMIC 28 nm.

→  Under preparation for ICCAD 2026. Technical details available upon request.
FFT Architecture DRAM Traffic Reduction Edge Radar RTL · Synthesis n-Dimensional
Target: ASAP 2026  ·  First Author Under Preparation

High-Energy-Efficiency FFT Vector Architecture with Stride-Aware Dataflow for Edge Computing

Proposes a custom vector accelerator for Radix-4 FFT targeting edge deployment. Key contributions include a combinational shuffle network physically coupled to the vector register file for zero-cycle data reorientation, and a stride-aware three-phase dataflow that fully decouples memory latency from arithmetic computation, achieving high energy efficiency with support for variable-length FFT.

→  Under preparation for ASAP 2026. Technical details available upon request.

Analog IC Design  ·  SCUT + Broadtek Completed

Full-Custom Analog IC: 555 Timer & LDO Regulator

Full-custom transistor-level design and verification of a 555 timer chip (tolerant-voltage CMOS process) on Cadence Virtuoso — including DRC/LVS-clean layout and three-corner post-simulation. Separately completed a full design-to-layout flow for an LDO regulator during an internship at Broadtek. Motivated by the long-term goal of mixed-signal compute-in-memory design.
